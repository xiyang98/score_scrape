<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Is this actually his violin concerto (published ca.1810 -see Letelllier's "Daniel-François-Esprit Auber: The Man and His Music"), merely transposed for cello by other hands? (If so, needs to be re-"instrumented" and re-tagged.) Incomplete parts are at BNF (see link) - "Fl, htb., 2 p. chacun, cl., 3 p., b.on, 4 p., le reste mq." (BNF has - at present undigitized? - this and the cello solo part of the 2nd cello concerto in edited/recopied manuscripts "d'après Riemann, édité sous le nom de&#160;: Jacques-Michel Hurel de Lamare".)
</p><p>(Where is "1809" from? Did he compose his first cello concerto at the same time as his first violin concerto was published, more or less?...)
</p><p>Auber did compose at least 3 cello concertos, though, and this might be one of them, if the tag Cello Concerto No.1 on the cover is accurate anyway. No.3 in B<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode; font-size:110%">&#x266d;</span>&#160;major (dedicated to Girod, and ms. found in the Belgian Royal Library) is described @ <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://opac.rism.info/metaopac/search?q=rism701001491&amp;db=251&amp;View=rism&amp;Language=en">B-Br Mus Ms 384/1</a></span>.
</p>

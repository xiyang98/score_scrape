<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Italian translation (as <i>Il Primo Giorno felice</i>) was published by <a href="/wiki/Escudier" title="Escudier">Escudier</a> in 1869 (Publication noted in the <i><a href="/wiki/Bibliographie_de_la_France" title="Bibliographie de la France">Bibliographie de la France</a></i>&#160;2e série, 58e année, Nº 11, 13 Mars 1869, p.130, r.885.), translated by A. (Achille?) de Lauzières. 
</p><p>French libretto shows up in BdlF in 1868.  Bazille's solo piano reduction (107p., see <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.worldcat.org/oclc/497392381">WorldCat</a></span>, so not the same as his vocal score) shows up in 1868 - Publication noted in the <i><a href="/wiki/Bibliographie_de_la_France" title="Bibliographie de la France">Bibliographie de la France</a></i>&#160;2e série, 57e année, Nº 19, 9 Mai 1868, p.221, r.1400.

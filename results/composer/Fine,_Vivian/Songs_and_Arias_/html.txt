<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>Commissioned by David Jolley
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Composer's note to the score:
</li></ul>
<dl><dd><i>Songs and Arias,</i> for French horn, violin and cello, is my most recent [instrumental] work based on vocal forms. <i>Lieder for Viola and Piano</i> was written in 1979, <i>Toccatas and Arias for Harpsichord</i> in 1986, <i>Toccatas and Arias for Piano</i> in 1987, <i>Madrigali Spirituali</i>, for trumpet and string quartet, in 1989. There is a wide range of moods in <i>Songs and Arias</i>: the elegiac mood of the Arioso, the intensity of Canto Hondo, the parodies Rupert’s Aria (from the non-existent opera “Unfulfilled”) and the Aria from the equally non-existent Cantata.
</dd></dl>

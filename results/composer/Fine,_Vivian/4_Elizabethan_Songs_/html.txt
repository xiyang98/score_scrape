<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>The songs have the same tonal approach as the Prelude for String Quartet….Keyboard gestures enhance the text….The writing is Rennaissance-like but in a contemporary setting. Fine never overwrites or uses cliches….frequently there is a surprising tonal subtlety….Humor is especially evident in ‘The Bargain.’ The vocal lines are well written and not complicated, and when necessary the piano provides pitch support.
</p><p>–Heidi Von Gunden, The Music of Vivian Fine, Scarecrow Press, 1999
</p>
<dl><dt>REVIEWS
</dt></dl>
<p>“Dirge (after Shakespeare)…a piece beautiful in its emotional depth and a calm, clear-eyed mastery mirroring an amazingly potent, fine intellect.”
</p><p>–Lazare Saminsky, Musical Courier, February 1, 1943
</p><p>“Four Elizabethan Songs…date from the end of the ‘30s, and as such are written in a more tonal style. Lois Stipp…communicated the alternately sombre and frolicking moods of these spare, economical art songs.”
</p><p>–Richard Chon, The Buffalo News, April 27, 1987
</p>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>A hymn…originally meant a form of “woven” or “spun” speech. Although the etymology is not Greek but Asiatic, the word derives from a Greek word for weaving, hyphainein.
</p>
<dl><dd><dl><dd><dl><dd>—from <i>The Homeric Hymns</i> by Charles Boer. Composer’s note to the score
</dd></dl>
</dd></dl>
</dd></dl>
<p>The idea of weaving reflects Fine's tendency to self-borrow. The first movement is from <i>Poetic Fires</i> and the second incorporates patterning and sketches from <i>Drama'</i>s Jealousy. Fine stated that since compositions were often heard only once, she had no objections to rewriting for different instrumentation.
</p>
<dl><dd><dl><dd><dl><dd>—Heidi Von Gunden, <i>The Music of Vivian Fine</i>, Scarecrow Press, 1999.
</dd></dl>
</dd></dl>
</dd></dl>

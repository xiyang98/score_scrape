<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>The Courante of this sonata is "borrowed" from the Courante of No.4 of <i><a href="/wiki/10_Inventions,_Op.10_(Bonporti,_Francesco_Antonio)" title="10 Inventions, Op.10 (Bonporti, Francesco Antonio)">10 Inventions</a></i>, Op.10, by <a href="/wiki/Category:Bonporti,_Francesco_Antonio" title="Category:Bonporti, Francesco Antonio">Francesco Antonio Bonporti</a>, published in 1712 .<br />
</li><li>According to WorldCat, this was originally from the violin sonatas, Book I no.11.  The Peters edition says "freely arranged" by P. Klengel.  There is no evidence that Eccles had anything to do with the viola arrangement, though it is widely known in this form.  Accordingly, this is tagged for violin, not viola.  Also, it is not known whether the Mutopia re-arrangement back to violin is accurate compared to the original, since "freely arranged" could include transposition to a lower key.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>It gets worse. According to Grove, Eccles was living in Paris (having moved there from London) by 1720, when he published the set of 12 violin sonatas. However, 18 of the movements of these sonatas are taken from <i><a href="/wiki/12_Allettamenti_da_Camera,_Op.8_(Valentini,_Giuseppe)" title="12 Allettamenti da Camera, Op.8 (Valentini, Giuseppe)">12 Allettamenti da Camera</a></i>, Op.8, by <a href="/wiki/Category:Valentini,_Giuseppe" title="Category:Valentini, Giuseppe">Giuseppe Valentini</a>, with another movement taken from <i><a href="/wiki/10_Inventions,_Op.10_(Bonporti,_Francesco_Antonio)" title="10 Inventions, Op.10 (Bonporti, Francesco Antonio)">10 Inventions</a></i>, Op.10, by <a href="/wiki/Category:Bonporti,_Francesco_Antonio" title="Category:Bonporti, Francesco Antonio">Francesco Antonio Bonporti</a> according to Grove. So who knows if this piece is even by Eccles? <a href="/wiki/User:Carolus" title="User:Carolus">Carolus</a> 19:46, 20 June 2012 (EDT)
</li></ul>

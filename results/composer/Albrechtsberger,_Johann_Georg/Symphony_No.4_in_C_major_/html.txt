The double bass part, in addition to being different from the cello part by having a less interesting melody to play during the cello solo, could also be made different by transposing up the low D that occurs at measure 35 in the slow movement.

<th>Instrumentation
</th>
<td>2 flutes, 2 oboes, bassoon, 2 horns, 2 trumpets, timpani, strings, cembalo

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Flutes are used only in the slow movement, but meant for independent players (that is to say, not the oboists). Certain features of the work suggests its origin as an oratorio overture.
</p>

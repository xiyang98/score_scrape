<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span></h3>
<ul><li>The composer Comire is identified as R.P. Comire on the cover page but as "le P. Comire" on the first page of volume 9. (Le Père Comire S. J., de la Société de Jésus ou Jésuite. R.P. is for "Révérend Père", the usual appellation.)
</li><li>The composer Lacaze is identified as J.L. Lacaze on the cover page but as J.F. Lacaze on the first page of volume 8. Identified on first page of volume 8 as "organiste de St. Pierre à Toulouse."
</li><li>Justin: organiste de Saint-Nazaire à Béziers (per BNF catalog and cover of volume 29).
</li><li>Léonce (Le Frère): According to Fetis (1881) a member of a religious order at Passy.
</li><li>A. Massis is Auguste Massis who was the "Organiste de St. Sernin à Toulouse" (identified only as Massis) who was the dedicatee of Lefébure-Wély's contribution in volume 11.
</li><li>J. Rolly: Ex-maître de chapelle de la métropole de Toulouse (according to cover page for volume 23).
</li><li>Jean Joseph Schluty: organiste de la Cathédrale d'Agen (according to cover page for volume 22).
</li><li>Alexandre Bruneau: organist at the Cathedral of St Etienne at Bourges.
</li></ul>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>According to Grove Music, the <i>Trumpet Voluntary</i> "is now known to have been a harpsichord piece entitled <i>The Prince of Denmark’s March</i>, which Clarke contributed to <i>A Choice Collection of Ayres</i> (RISM 1700¹0). As it also survives in a suite of pieces by Clarke (GB-Lbl Add.30839 and 39565–7), it may even be that, unlike many similar harpsichord pieces written in imitation of the trumpet style, it actually began life as a piece for trumpet and strings."  The suite that Grove cites is for winds (though they are not specified).
</li><li>This has been tagged for harpsichord for the moment, since its earliest published form is that, but it has not been tagged for the wind suite, since that merely contains the voluntary as a movement inside the suite.  A link can be added between the two pages, should the suite ever be uploaded.  The trumpet and strings version is speculatory, so that version has not been tagged.  It can be added if evidence ever surfaces that Clarke wrote such a version.
</li></ul>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li><b>Original Version</b>
</li></ul>
<dl><dd>Originally published in four volumes of 4, 4, 4, and 3 song, as well as in a single "Sonder-Ausgabe." In both cases the songs are numbered I.a-d, II.a-d, III.a-d, and IV.a-c. A 1951 reprint removes the numbering, but preserves the groupings on the title page. Songs are numbered 1 through 15 in the autograph score. The 1951 edition lacks the BSS numbers. Revised in 1935-48 (see new version below).
<dl><dd><i>-Paul Hindemith: A Research and Information Guide</i>
</dd></dl>
</dd></dl>
<ul><li><b>New Version</b>
</li></ul>
<dl><dd>Thoroughly revised 1935-48; some of the songs revised multiple times; only No.12 not revised. Dates below are from Hindemith's work lists, and represent only some of the revisions. He considered the revision complete in March 1941 after work on number 9 and one of the "Vom Tode MariÃ¤" songs (confusingly identified as the second of these but no. the recomposiition of "MariÃ¤ VerkÃ¼ndigung."  
</dd><dd>Includes extensive introductory remarks by the composer (English translation, by Arthur Mendel, also published seperately by AMP, 1953).
<dl><dd><i>-Paul Hindemith: A Research and Information Guide</i>
</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>Soprano and Orchestra Arrangement</i>
</li></ul>
<dl><dd>Orchestrations of songs of the Marienleben cycle (q.v.), in all cases based on revised version, although those of Nos.1-4 are not always identical to the final 1948 version.
<dl><dd><i>-Paul Hindemith: A Research and Information Guide</i>

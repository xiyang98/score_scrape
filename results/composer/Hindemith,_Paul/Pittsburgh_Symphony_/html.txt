<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>Finale includes a quotation from Anton Webern's symphony Op.21 (not known to be a favorite of Hindemith's, I think- at least, Hindemith was regarded as not being a "fan" of music written using serial "techniques".)
</li></ul>
<ul><li>The work also quotes (is better-known for quoting, at that) the folk-tune "Hab[e] lumbedruwwel mit me lumbeschatz". - ES
</li></ul>
<ul><li>"Paul Hindemith wrote the Pittsburgh Symphony, his sixth and final symphony, at the request of the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra's then-music director William Steinberg in 1958 to celebrate the 200th anniversary of the founding of the city." - from James Leonard's description at All Music Guide
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Movements when written
</li></ul>
<dl><dd>I. Molto energico (1958-08-09)
</dd><dd>II. Slow March (1958-09-09)
</dd><dd>III. Ostinato. - Allegro moderato (1958-11-13)
</dd></dl>

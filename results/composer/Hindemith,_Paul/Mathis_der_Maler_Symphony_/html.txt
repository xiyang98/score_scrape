<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>Music subsequently used in the opera of the same name (q.v.) Plate no. BSS 34000a was apparently first used for the autograph facsimile (65p.), according to Stephen Hinton in <i>SW</i> (SÃ¤mtliche Werke) 11/2 (p. 184), but was later applied to a 100-page full score as well. The edition no. 3509 (Schott London: 3509a) and plate number BSS 34052 have been applied to both the 90 and 100-page miniature scores.
</li></ul>
<dl><dd>-<i>Paul Hindemith: A Research and Information Guide</i> 
</dd></dl>
<ul><li>From the Opera of the same name, each movement come from the following parts of the Opera:
</li></ul>
<dl><dd>I. Engelkonzert (The overture)
</dd><dd>II. Grablegung (Orchestral interlude from the last act)
</dd><dd>III. Versuchung des heiligen Antonius (Orchestral piece reworked and amplified into a visionary scene in Scene 6 of the Opera)
</dd><dd><i>-Cited from Wikipedia</i>
</dd></dl>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Hindemith's title is as above. German editions have the titles <i>Sinfonische Metamorphosen nach Themen von Carl Maria von Weber</i> or <i>Sinfonische Metamorphosen Carl Maria von Weber'scher Themen</i>; numerous further variants and misspellings in other sources that were found. In 1940, Hindemith and Massine had discussed plans for a ballet using Weber's music, and in March Hindemith made piano scores of Weber's Op. 60 No. 4 and Op. 10 No. 2 --"die Weber Musik leicht gefärbt und ein bißchen schärfer gemacht" (Hindemith to Getrud Hindemith, letter of 12 April 1940)--the pieces that eventually served as the basis of the first and third movements, respectively, of the <i>Symphonic Metamorphosis</i>. The ballet project was not realized, in part because Massine wanted strict arrangements of the Weber pieces. Actually staged as a ballet by Georges Balanchine and his New York City Ballet, 25 Novemeber 1952.
</p>
<dl><dd>-<i>Paul Hindemith: A Research and Information Guide</i>
</dd></dl>

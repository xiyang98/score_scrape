<p><br />
</p>
<ul><li> For his recording of 1987, Matthew Best used a copy of the dedication <i>Reinschrift</i>, which Bruckner made for Ignaz von Assmayr. This <i>Reinschrift</i> is still in a private collection in Vienna. 
</li><li> The edition Reuter is based on an <i>Arbeitspartitur</i>. On the first half of bar 109 (word "Tode"), the trombones accompaniment is missing. There are also a few other differences versus the more complete <i>Reinschrift</i>.
</li><li>A copy of the <i>Reinschrift</i> has also been used by Paul Hawkshaw in 1997 for issuing the score of the Bruckner <i>Gesamtausgabe</i>. The other recording by Johannes Rühl (1999) used the score of the <i>Gesamtausgabe</i>.
</li></ul>

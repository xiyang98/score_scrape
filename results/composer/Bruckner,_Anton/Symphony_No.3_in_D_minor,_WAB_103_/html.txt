<th>Misc. Notes
</th>
<td>1873 1st version; includes revisions made in 1874.<br />Bruckner completed the Third Symphony on 31 December 1873 and had two copy scores made: (a) the Wagner dedication score and (b) <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://data.onb.ac.at/rec/AL00158770">Mus.Hs. 6033</a></span> in the Austrian National Library. The 1873 version was rehearsed by the Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra in June or July 1874, but not accepted for performance. The 1873 version was lost in Leipzig during World War II. <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://data.onb.ac.at/rec/AL00158770">Mus.Hs. 6033</a></span> contains the 1874 version and includes revisions made in 1874. See <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.bruckner.webs.com/versions.html#sym3">Griegel</a></span>.

<th>Misc. Notes
</th>
<td>Bruckner revised the symphony again from January through April 1877. Based on dates in the score, we know that Bruckner revised the Finale during this time; he may or may not have revised the other movements also. Bruckner revised the Adagio in October 1877, then revised the Scherzo in January 1878, which included the addition of a 41-bar coda, and this represents the final form of the autograph manuscript, now <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://data.onb.ac.at/rec/AL00218758">Mus.Hs.19475</a></span> in the Austrian National Library, which is one source for the score published in 1878.

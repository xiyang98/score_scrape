<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Second(?) edition of the concerto (ca.1810: London: Gianchetti &amp; Sperati) was published as "Grand Symphony Concertante for two Piano Fortes" (plate no.11), according to Lindeman (<i>Structural Novelty and Tradition...</i>, appendix, p.230.) (According to Worldcat, this edition was "Published after the Parisian edition [the Pleyel] done under the direction of the author, by Cianchettini &amp; Sperati" and if so, the title probably also met with the approval with the composer as a permissible alternate. If anything, "concertos" should be removed from the tags if a use of "concerto" to describe the work with Dussek's approval or even pre-1813 can't be found. Scan of London print (also first piano part only, unfortunately-- however, also piano solo parts of many of Dussek's other piano concertos, which could be used to at least begin workpages for them if not done already...): see <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://archive.org/stream/concertosforpian00duss#page/n305/mode/1up">Dussek <i>Concertos for the pianoforte</i>, Thematic Index, and Solo Parts - 2 Piano Concerto, Primo Part</a></span>.
</p>
<dl><dd>Is there any evidence that the first edition (named "Concerto") did not have Dussek's approval?
</dd></dl>
<p><br />
Instrumentation of orchestral parts (first edition) at BNF:<br />"Piano 1°, 2°, violino 1°, 2°, viola, violoncello, controbasso, flauto 1°, 2°, façetto 1°, 2°, corno 1°, 2°, clarinetto 1°, 2°" - 2 flutes, 2 clarinets, 2 "façetto" (?), 2 horns, strings. (façetto- fagotto- bassoons? or something else?...) See <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://catalogue.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/cb42971849j/PUBLIC">BNF</a></span>. (Cotage/plate also 780.)
</p>

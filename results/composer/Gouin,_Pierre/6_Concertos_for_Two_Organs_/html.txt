</th>
<td>1770&#160;?

<tr>
<th>First Pub<span class="mh555">lication</span><span class="ms555">.</span>
</th>
<td>1952 (to 1962) in <i>Musica Hispana II, Serie C</i>, Spanish Institute of Musicology (Barcelona), out of print.
</td></tr>
<tr>
<th>Copyright Information
</th>
<td>An excerpt of the second movement of the <i>Concierto VI</i> (Minué and variations) was widely popularized in an arrangement for organ solo by E. Power Biggs (1906-1977). As <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.worldcat.org/oclc/498299602">this record</a></span> indicates, it was first published in 1969 and was <i>Freely adapted, arranged</i> by Biggs from Soler's original. The Biggs arrangement is under copyright worldwide and cannot be added here until January 1, 2028. It will be under copyright in the USA until 2065 and in the EU until 2048. The title <i>Fanfare impériale</i> or <i>Imperial Fanfare</i> is not present in the composer's original score. The above arrangement by Pierre Gouin entitled <i>Toccata Real</i> and its orchestration by Michel Rondeau are <i>not</i> based upon the Biggs arrangement in any way, but created independently from Soler's original score.
</td></tr>
<tr>
<th>Dedication
</th>
<td><i>Para la diversion del SSmo. Infante de España Dn. Gabriel de Bourbón.</i>
</td></tr>
<tr>
<th><span class="mh555">Composer Time Period</span><span class="ms555">Comp. Period</span>

</td></tr>
<tr>
<th>Piece Style
</th>
<td><a href="/wiki/Category:Classical_style" title="Category:Classical style">Classical</a>
</td></tr>
<tr>
<th>Instrumentation
</th>
<td>2 organs, harpsichords or pianos
</td></tr>
<tr>
<th>Extra Information
</th>
<td>The wide compass used by Soler in these <i>Conciertos</i>, exceeds - in the bass notably - the usual organ keyboard and makes them more suitable for 2 harpsichords or pianofortes.
</td></tr></table>

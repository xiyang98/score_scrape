<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>According to Wikipedia, "The famous <i>Trumpet Tune in D</i> (also incorrectly attributed to Purcell) was taken from the semi-opera <i>The Island Princess</i>, which was a joint musical production of Clarke and Daniel Purcell (Henry Purcell's younger brother)probably leading to the confusion."  Their sources are Bette Matthews (2004) Wedding for All Seasons p. 119. Barnes &amp; Noble Publishing, 2004, and Cudworth, C., &amp; Zimmerman, F. B. (1960). The trumpet voluntary. Music and Letters, 41:341348 (see p.347).
</li><li>This may have to be re-tagged if music for <i>The Island Princess</i> shows a different instrumentation involving an orchestra.
</li></ul>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>At the end of the manuscript score Georg Gerson describes the circumstances around the piece. It was composed for an Italian tenor singer, <i>Giovanni Piantanida</i> (Gaetano Piantanida 1768-1836) visiting Copenhagen 1813 for a concert at <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.kb.dk/da/nb/tema/musikteater/Musikalsk_byvandring/Odd_Fellow_Palaeet">The Odd Fellow Palace</a></span>. On the planned program was an aria, <i>Sento mancarmi l'anima</i> which Gerson ascribed to <a href="/wiki/Category:Mayr,_Giovanni_Simone" title="Category:Mayr, Giovanni Simone">Giovanni Simone Mayr</a>. This aria was most likely written by <a href="/wiki/Category:Crescentini,_Girolamo" title="Category:Crescentini, Girolamo">Girolamo Crescentini</a> (1762-1846) as an addition to Mayr's opera <i>Alonso e Cora</i>. Gerson used a motive from the aria in the music to follow, the present recitativo and aria with text by <a href="/wiki/Category:Vestris,_Stefano" title="Category:Vestris, Stefano">Stefano Vestris</a> taken from a cantata, <i><a href="/wiki/Diana_e_Endimione_(Pa%C3%ABr,_Ferdinando)" title="Diana e Endimione (Par, Ferdinando)">Diana e Endimione</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Category:Pa%C3%ABr,_Ferdinando" title="Category:Par, Ferdinando">Ferdinando Par</a>. In the end the concert was dropped and the planned composition was not completed. Instead Gerson wrote a sketch to a short coda to be used for a stand alone performance of the recitativo and aria.
</p>

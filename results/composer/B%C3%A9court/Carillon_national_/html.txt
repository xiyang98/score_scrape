<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>According to Oxford Music, the well-known French revolutionary song <i>Ah, ça ira</i> (1790) adopted a tune from a popular contredanse.  The contredanse was known as <i>Carillon national</i>, but when "Ça ira" is sung to it, it becomes the revolutionary song.  Also according to Oxford Music, it accompanied "almost every incident of the Terror".
</li></ul>
<dl><dd>aka: "Ah&#160;! Ça ira, dictum populaire, air du carillon national". Published ca.1790, indeed, as "Le retour du champs de Mars. Air du carillon national, [texte par M. Déduit]" according to Worldcat. BNF also has, attributed to Bécourt, "Suitte de (followup to, not suite of?- ES) Ça ira l'aristocratie en déroute. Air du carillon national." - ES
</dd></dl>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p><b>N.B.</b> The 4-shape notation here is Law's own invention - in addition to being printed without staff lines, it differs from the more common 4-shape system that is still in use today (first used in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Easy_Instructor_(Various)" title="The Easy Instructor (Various)">The Easy Instructor</a></i>) in that the rectangle notehead denotes <b>faw</b>, and the triangle <b>law</b>. He later revised this so that 7 syllables could be used, by distinguishing three shapes with a dot (looking like a staccato dot), in effect a sort of 7-shape notation.
<br />
</p>
<ul><li>Full titles (2nd edition):
</li></ul>
<ol><li> The Musical Primer; or the First Part of the Art of Singing: Containing the Rules of Psalmody, Newly Revised and Improved; Together with a number of Practical Lessons and Plain Tunes: Designed expressly for the use of Learners. 
</li><li> The Christian Harmony; or the Second Part of the Art of Singing: Comprising a Select Variety of Psalm and Hymn Tunes; together with a number of Airs and Anthems: Calculated for Schools and Churches. In Two Volumes. 
</li><li> The Musical Magazine; containing a variety of favorite pieces. A Periodical Publication.
</li></ol>

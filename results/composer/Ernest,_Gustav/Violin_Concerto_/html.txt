<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Parts are at the Library of Congress it seems (<span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://lccn.loc.gov/unk84162784">link</a></span>.)
</p><p>According to Gerhard Heldt, <i>Das deutsche nachromantische Violinkonzert...</i>, Ernest wrote two violin concertos of which this is the first.
</p><p>Premiere(?): <i>Signale für die musikalische Welt</i>, 67. Jährg. No. 8, p.300 (1909) reports on an English concert in which the composer (resident in England at the time, if I read correctly) conducted this concerto (and Wessely played violin).  This took place in "Sydenham Conservatory".
</p>

<td>2 Violins, Viola, Cello and Double bass (2nd Cello in the first edition); the first edition by Pleyel contains also an "Alto-Violoncello"-Part (= 2nd Viola for 1st Cello), which was probably written by Pleyel himself. This part is included also in Enrico Polo's edition of G.339 (Op.39/3).
</td></tr>
<tr>
<th>Extra Information
</th>
<td>First published as Op.37/20, Op.47/4, Op.37/23
</td></tr></table>
</div>
<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Grove Music gives the original as 2 violins, viola, cello, and double bass, with no alternative instrumentation.
</p>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>One of the most important works in the Spanish piano literature. <i>Iberia</i> is a wonderful mixture of Spanish music with impressionist influences. It is probably one of the most dificult Spanish works, with Granados' Goyescas or Falla's Fantasía Baetica.
</p><p>In the first book, we find <i>Evocación</i>, a little easy introduction to Iberia; <i>El Puerto</i> - de Cádiz? - [The Cadiz Port], a very funny <i>zapateado</i> - an Andalusian dance; and the impressive <i>El Corpus en Sevilla</i> [The Corpus-Christi in Seville], a musical version of the story of these celebrations.
</p><p>The <i>Rondeña</i> is the first dance in Iberia's second book. It is based in <i>peteneras</i>, Andalusian dances with continuous metric change - 3/4 and 6/8. After that comes the famous <i>Almería</i> with the copla based in a <i>fandango</i> - a Spanish dance. <i>Triana</i> - a popular Sevillian district - is probably the most brilliant piece in the whole collection. It is a fireworks' spectacle of Andalusian essence with an important <i>gitana</i> influence.
</p><p>In <i>Albaicín</i>, Albéniz expresses his vision of Granada's gyspy district. It has more melancholy than any other piece in Iberia. <i>El Polo</i> is a <i>palo flamenco</i>, a flamenco dance in which the <i>cantaor</i> sings with a supernatural anguish. <i>Lavapiés</i>, not "feet washing" but one of the most popular Madrilenian districts, is a <i>habanera</i> - a Spanish dance with Cuban origins - devoid of Spanish frivolities, but with an apotheosis of <i>lo castizo</i>.
The spectacular <i>Málaga</i> is full of movement so typical of the malagueñas - Andalusian dances in flamenco style. It demands a completely overwhelming virtuosity. In <i>Jerez</i> - a little Spanish city between Huelva and Portugal with an important agricultural industry -, Albéniz gives us one of the most exquisite coplas with a dense compositional texture. Finally, the brilliant and happy <i>Eritaña</i> has a determination that hardly requires further interpretation.
</p>

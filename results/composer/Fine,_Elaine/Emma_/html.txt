<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>Emma is a two-act opera based on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emma_(play)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Emma (play)">play</a> with the same name by Howard Zinn (revised in 1987) about the American Anarchist <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emma_Goldman" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Emma Goldman">Emma Goldman</a> (1869–1940). The narrative of the opera is woven from relationships and events in Goldman’s autobiography Living My Life. It is at once a story of a passionately-driven social and political reformer, and the story of a woman torn between her feminist ideals and her vulnerability in matters of love. The action of the opera begins 1887 when Emma is an 18-year-old factory worker in Rochester and ends in 1917 when, at age 48, she is about to be exiled to Russia. The music is mainly tonal with a musical color that often evokes the Yiddish theatre of the early 20th century.
</li></ul>

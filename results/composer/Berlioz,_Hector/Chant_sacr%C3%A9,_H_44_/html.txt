<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Adapted from a section (the <i>Hymne</i>) of the 1828 Prix d’Rome cantata, <i><a href="/wiki/Herminie,_H_29_(Berlioz,_Hector)" title="Herminie, H 29 (Berlioz, Hector)"><i>Herminie</i></a></i>. The lost version of 1844 utilised six wind instruments by Belgian instrument maker <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adolphe_Sax" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Adolphe Sax">Adolphe Sax</a>, and represents the first known use of the saxophone (David Cairns, Berlioz: <i>Servitude and Greatness</i>, p.299)
</p>
<h3> <span class="mw-headline" id="Publication_History">Publication History</span></h3>
<ul><li>Paris: January 1830, as Œuvre 2, No.6 (vocal score)
</li><li>Paris: 1863, <b>H 44A</b> included in <i>Collection de 32 Mélodies</i>: no. 12

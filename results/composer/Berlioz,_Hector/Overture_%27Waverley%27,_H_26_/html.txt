<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Loosely based on Berlioz’s impressions of the novel by Sir <a href="/wiki/Category:Scott,_Walter" title="Category:Scott, Walter">Walter Scott</a> (1771–1832) and bearing a quotation “Dreams of love and Lady’s charms/Give place to honour and to arms”, the work was dedicated to the composer’s maternal uncle, who may have played a part in his earliest publications dating from the late 1810s/early 1820s.
</p>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="Publication_History">Publication History</span></h4>
<ul><li>Paris: 1839, Œuvre 1 (full score)
</li><li>Leipzig: Breitkopf und Härtel, n.d. (1901), as part of Hector Berlioz Werke (1900–07), Serie II, Band V, edited by Charles Malherbe and Felix Weingartner
</li><li>Kassel: Bärenreiter, 2000, BA 5460, as part of New Berlioz Edition (1967–2005), Volume 20, edited by Diana Bickley
</li></ul>

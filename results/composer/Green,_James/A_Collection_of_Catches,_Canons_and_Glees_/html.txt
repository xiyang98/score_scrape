<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>"... some of the most exquisite gems of vocal composition ever <br />
written, together with productions of so questionable, or rather <br />
unquestionable a character, that it is a pity that the fire which <br />
consumed the greater portion of the stock of copies did not <br />
consume the whole."  W.A. Barrett (1886), p. 209. <br />
</p><p>"... the proportion of wheat to chaff is remarkably high. There is <br />
barely a page that is not a delight to sing.... The music of the  <br />
<i>Warren Collection</i> ... is wasted languishing in rare editions on <br />
a few library shelves. It rather deserves renewed study and performance <br />
as a monument of all that was best in Georgian culture. The spirit that <br />
will bear most fruit in approaching these pieces is embodied in the <br />
motto of the Noblemen and Gentlemen's Catch Club: 'Let's drink and <br />
let's sing together.'"  Emanuel Rubin, from his Introduction to the <br />
1970 Facsimile Edition. <br /><br />
</p><p>Thomas Warren was the founding Secretary of the Noblemen and Gentlemen's Catch Club.<br />
These volumes contain compositions that he selected and published from among entries<br />
in the Catch Club's prize competitions.
</p>

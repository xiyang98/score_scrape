<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li> This fugue was previously thought to have been arranged from a work by <a href="/wiki/Category:Erselius,_Johann_Christoph" title="Category:Erselius, Johann Christoph">Johann Christoph Erselius</a>. More recent analysis by Karl Heller ascertained that: "The B-flat major fugue, BWV 955, has hitherto been considered an arrangement by Bach of a G-major fugue by Johann Christopher Erselius (ca.1703-72), a hypothesis now disproven by source and textual studies. The G-major version is actually a transposition of an early version (BWV 955a) of the B-flat major fugue; both date from Bach's early years as a composer" â 'Die Klavierfuge BWV 955: Zur Frage ihres Autors und ihrer verschiedenen Fassungen', <i>Das Fruhwerk Johann Sebastian Bachs: Kolloquium veranstaltet vom Institut fur Musikwissenschaft der Universitat Rostock, 11.-13. September 1990</i> (Koln: Studio, 1995) p.130â141.
</li></ul>
<ul><li><ul><li> Both BWV 955a, 955 discussed (as works  in volume 3 of Schulenberg's 2013 "The Keyboard Music of J.S. Bach" - <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://books.google.com/books?id=VaYCK7-In9YC&amp;pg=PA67">brief BWV955 analysis and discussion</a></span>. (Schulenberg notes that it is not, <i>pace</i>!! Heller's statement, regarded as a matter quite disproven, but Schulenberg has no problem with 955 being accepted as part of the Bach canon - on the one hand "it will appear in the N[eue]B[ach]A[usgabe] reserved for doubtful clavier works", on the other, "less well documented works have been accepted into the Bach canon".
</li></ul>

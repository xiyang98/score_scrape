<th><span class="mh555">Movements/Sections</span><span class="ms555">Mov'ts/Sec's</span>
</th>
<td>14 pieces
<ol><li> Canon simplex (simple canon)
</li><li> Canon all' roverscio (canon in inversion)
</li><li> Canon in motu recto e contrario<br />(both previous canons simultaneously / the subject followed by its inversion) 
</li><li> Canon in motu contrario e recto<br />(the inversion followed by subject)
</li><li> Canon duplex à 4 (double canon in 4 parts)
</li><li> Canon simplex à 3 über besagtes Fundament<br />(simple canon in 3 parts over said foundation)
</li><li> Canon simplex à 3 (the same [as Canon 6] in 3 parts)
</li><li> Canon simplex à 3. il soggetto in Alto<br />(simple canon in 3 parts with the subject in alto)
</li><li> Canon in unisono post semifusam à 3<br />(canon in unison after a semiquaver in 3 parts)
</li><li> Canon à 2 Alio modo per syncopationes et per ligaturas<br />(canon in 2 parts in another manner by syncopation and merging)
</li><li> Canon duplex übers Fundament à 5<br />(double canon over the foundation in 5 parts)
</li><li> Canon duplex über Fundamental Noten à 5<br />(double canon over the fundamental notes in 5 parts)
</li><li> Canon triplex à 6 (triple canon in 6 parts)
</li><li> Canon à 4 per Augmentationem et Diminutionem<br />(canon in 4 parts by augmentation and diminution)
</li></ol>
</td></tr>

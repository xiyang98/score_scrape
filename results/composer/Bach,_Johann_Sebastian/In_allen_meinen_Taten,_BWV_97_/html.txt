<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Unknown occasion. 
</p>
<h4> <span class="mw-headline" id="Commentary_on_the_movements">Commentary on the movements</span></h4>
<dl><dd>Versus 1. Chorus: In allen meinen Taten. A french overture to begin. The slow section at the beginning is instrumental; the chorale is then added to the fugal section. 
</dd><dd>Versus 2. Aria (Bass): Nichts ist es spat und frühe. A continuo aria with a rather vocal main theme. 
</dd><dd>Versus 3. Recitativo (Tenor): Es kann mir nichts geschehen. Secco recitative.
</dd><dd>Versus 4. Aria (Tenor): Ich traue seiner Gnaden. A highly concertante solo violin part adds vigour to this movement. 
</dd><dd>Versus 5. Recitativo (Alto): Er wolle meiner Sünden. Recitative with string accompaniment. 
</dd><dd>Versus 6. Aria (Alto): Leg ich mich späte nieder. Fully concerned with illustrating the text, this movement is full of the contrasts contained therein. 
</dd><dd>Versus 7. Duetto (Soprano, Bass): Hat er es denn beschlossen. A duet with continuo alone. One of the more relaxed and "enjoyable" movements in the piece.
</dd><dd>Versus 8. Aria (Soprano): Ihm hab ich mich ergeben. Two obbligato oboes with rather difficult parts accompany another relaxed movement. 
</dd><dd>Versus Ultimus. Choral: So sei nun, Seele, deine. A plain chorale setting with obbligato strings. 
</dd></dl>

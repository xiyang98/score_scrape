<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Note on scoring: The second choir acts as a ripieno ensemble, doubling their counterparts in the first choir at certain points. These are marked in the Choir I parts using the terms "solo" and "tutti". Coro I voices must be considered soloists ("conc." in the index), and Coro II, as a chorus (dispensable according to the index). Organ II is not a separate part but doubles the "Organus" as a reinforcement when Coro II is singing. For certain pieces with 5 and 8 voices solo, the solo parts are found in the Coro II part books. Pieces with 5 soloists feature a second Soprano (Coro II: Soprano) and also ad lib. reinforced choir from Coro II (both sopranos are in unison for the "tutti" sections). In pieces for 8 voices, all voices (Coro I and II) have separate parts and must be considered all soloists. Violins are also ad lib. for some pieces ("con doi viol. &amp; il 2. Choro Ã  beneplacito)
</p>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Written for - or written earlier and incorporated in - the ode "Alexander's Feast" HWV 75 (which was premiered February 19, 1736). ("In its original form it contained three concertos: a concerto in B flat major in 3 movements for "Harp, Lute, Lyrichord and other Instruments" HWV 294 for performance after the recitative Timotheus, plac'd on high in Part I; a concerto grosso in C major in 4 movements for oboes, bassoon and strings, now known as the "Concerto in Alexander's Feast" HWV 318, performed between Parts I and II; and an organ concerto HWV 289 in G minor and major in 4 movements for chamber organ, oboes, bassoon and strings performed after the chorus Let old Timotheus yield the prize in Part II. - Wikipedia)
</p><p>Apparently published by Longman &amp; Broderip ca.1791, possibly earlier.
</p>

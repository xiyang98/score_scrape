<th>Extra Information
</th>
<td><i>(From the wikipedia article)</i>
<dl><dd>Variously described as a serenata, a masque, a pastoral or pastoral opera, a "little opera" (in a letter by the composer while it was being written), an entertainment and (in the New Grove Dictionary) as an oratorio. The work was originally devised as a one-act masque which premiered in 1718. Handel later adapted the piece into a three-act serenata for the Italian opera troupe in London in 1732, which incorporated a number of songs (still in Italian) from <i><a href="/wiki/Aci,_Galatea_e_Polifemo,_HWV_72_(Handel,_George_Frideric)" title="Aci, Galatea e Polifemo, HWV 72 (Handel, George Frideric)">Aci, Galatea e Polifemo, HWV 72</a></i>, his 1708 setting of the same story to different music. He later adapted the original English work into its two-act form in 1739.
</dd></dl>

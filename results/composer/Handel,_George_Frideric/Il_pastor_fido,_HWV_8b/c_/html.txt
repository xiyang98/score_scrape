<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>"In the spring of 1734... Handel revived <i>Il pastor fido</i> in a much changed version [HWV 8b], preserving the outlines of the story but retaining only seven of the original arias; the rest (except for a new setting of Mirtilloâs opening cavatina) were taken from cantatas or earlier operas, with the significant addition of choruses taken from the serenata Parnasso in Festa... This version was the basis of yet another revision [HWV 8c] which opened Handelâs first season at John Richâs new theatre in Covent Garden on 9 November 1734. There were two new arias... but the main changes were prompted by Richâs engagement of a dance troupe led by Marie SallÃ©. For her benefit Handel added a new prologue, <i>Terpsicore</i>, a mixture of vocal solos, choruses and orchestral dance movements (some taken from earlier works) in which Terpsichore, the Muse of Dance (played by SallÃ©), is encouraged by Erato (soprano) and Apollo (mezzo-soprano) to demonstrate her talents...Newly composed dances were also added at the end of each act of the opera" (Source: <i>The New Grove Dictionary of Opera</i>).
</p>

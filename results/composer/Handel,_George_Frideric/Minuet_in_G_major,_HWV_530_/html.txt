<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>Grove Music's work list for Handel does not mention HWV 530 specifically, except as a comment that the song "Bacchus one day gaily striding" (Bacchus’ Speech in Praise of Wine) was constructed with "words added to authentic inst minuet (hwv530); tune is Air XVII in C. Coffey: The Devil to Pay, Aug 1731".
</li><li>Tagged for harpsichord, since, according to Grove Music, Handel's keyboard music was "all probably for harpsichord and written before 1720, unless otherwise stated".
</li></ul>

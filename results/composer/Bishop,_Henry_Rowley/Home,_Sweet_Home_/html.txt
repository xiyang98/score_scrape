<p>1. 'Mid scenes of confusion and creature complaints,<br />
How sweet to the soul is communion with saints!<br />
To find at the banquet of mercy there's room,<br />
And feel in the presence of Jesus at home.<br />
<small><i>Chorus</i></small><br />
Home! home! sweet, sweet home!<br />
Prepare me, dear Savior, for glory, my home.<br /><br />
2. Sweet bonds that unite all the children of peace!<br />   
And, thrice precious, Jesus, whose love cannot cease,<br />

I long to behold thee in glory, at home.<br /><br />

The Spirit's sure witness, and smiles of thy face;<br />

And find, even now, a sweet foretaste of home.<br /><br />

Receive me, dear Savior, in glory, my home.<br /><br />

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>Although Bishop composed this song, it was adapted from John Howard Payne's 1823 opera Clari, Maid of Milan.  The melody is Bishop's, the text is Payne's.  The song itself is not an extract from Payne's opera.
<br />
It is interesting, in comparing different versions, to see how the melody has altered widely through the folk process.<br /><br />
Also see the discussion of this song in <a href="/wiki/The_Works_of_Sir_Henry_Bishop_(Corder,_Frederick)" title="The Works of Sir Henry Bishop (Corder, Frederick)">The Works of Sir Henry Bishop</a> (last few pages of that article).
</p>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p><b>La Niña de Guatemala</b>
</p><p>Quiero, a la sombra de un ala, / contar este cuento en flor: / La niña de Guatemala, / la que se murió de amor.
</p><p>Eran de lirio los ramos, / y las orlas de reseda / y de jazmín; la enterramos / en una caja de seda.
</p><p>…Ella dio al desmemoriado / una almohadilla de olor; / él volvió, volvió casado; / ella se murió de amor.
</p><p>Iban cargándola en andas / obispos y embajadores; / detrás iba el pueblo en tandas, / todo cargado de flores.
</p><p>…Ella, por volverlo a ver, / salió a verlo al mirador: / él volvió con su mujer; / ella se murió de amor.
</p><p>Como de bronce candente / al beso de despedida / era su frente ¡la frente / que más he amado en mi vida!
</p><p>…Se entró de tarde en el río, / la sacó muerta el doctor; / dicen que murió de frío: / yo sé que murió de amor.
</p><p>Allí, en la bóveda helada, / la pusieron en dos bancos; / besé su mano afilada, / besé sus zapatos blancos.
</p><p>Callado, al obscurecer, / me llamó el enterrador. / ¡Nunca más he vuelto a ver / a la que murió de amor!
</p><p><b>The Girl from Guatemala</b>
</p><p>At a wing’s shade, / I want to tell this story, like a flower: / The girl from Guatemala, / the girl that died of love.
</p><p>The flowers were lilies, / and mignonette ornaments / and jasmine; we buried her / in a silk casket.
</p><p>…She gave to the forgetful / a perfumed sachet: / he came back, came back married; / she died of love.
</p><p>She was carried in a procession / by bishops and ambassadors; / behind were the town’s people in groups / they were all carrying flowers.
</p><p>…She, wanted to see him again, / she stepped out to the balcony: / he came back with his wife; / she died of love.
</p><p>Like ardent bronze, / when he kissed her goodbye, / her forehead was the forehead / that I have loved the most in my life!
</p><p>…She went into the river at dusk, / she was dead when the doctor pulled her out: / some say she died of coldness: / but I know she died of love.
</p><p>There, in the chilling crypt, / they set her on two benches: / I kissed her slender hand, / I kissed her white shoes.
</p><p>Silently, when it grew dark, / the undertaker called me: / I have never again seen / the girl who died of love!
</p>

<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<p>If one understands the notes to a Verso recording of two concertos by Goldberg properly, then there are only 2 surviving harpsichord concertos by him (in D minor and E-flat), and so the D minor concerto recorded there must be the one here. Both concertos are -very- substantial in length (and perhaps this composer is more on the early Classical side than the Baroque side of the line in some ways if not others) (even if date is "1751-1776"(?).)
</p><p>RISM also has a D minor concerto DürG 16a which seems to be an incomplete different concerto in D minor by the composer.
</p>

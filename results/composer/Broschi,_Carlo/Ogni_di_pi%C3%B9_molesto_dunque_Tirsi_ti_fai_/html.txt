<h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>This aria and recitativo comes from Nicola Conforto's opera <i>Le Cinesi</i>, performed in Madrid in 1751 as <i>Festa cinese</i>.  This probably is the only part Broschi wrote.
</li><li>IMSLP does not currently have the rest of the opera, but if we eventually do get it uploaded, a link needs to be made between this page and that one.
</li><li>The vocal part is in C clef.  Carlo Broschi's more familiar name is Farinelli, probably the most famed castrato in history.  One can perhaps assume the recitive and aria was written for himself.
</li></ul>

As these comments are dated in months only, prior to that year's looming Christmas music making festivities, it is unfortunately not possible to date the publication precisely. <h2> <span class="mw-headline" id="Misc._Comments">Misc. Comments</span></h2>
<ul><li>Incidental to this collection is its editor's second name, a quite extraordinary one it would seem in the Victorian era, but it should be construed with propriety, as is established <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.surnamedb.com/Surname/Tiplady">here</a>. 
</li><li>At least part of this collection appears to have been printed in Germany (C.G.Röder, Leipzig). The volume 3 layout for instance corresponds to the "unidentified publisher" mentioned on the imslp Kalliwoda page under the heading <i>3 Easy Duos, Op.179</i> &amp; <i>3 Easy Duos op.180</i>. The Viotti Duets are less easy to identify but can be found in the volume of bound manuscripts held in the Kongelige Bibliothek which has been added to imslp.<br />It's also similar to Weiss, Jul., <i>Instruktive Violin-Duette in stufenweiser Schwierigkeitsfolge</i> (6 vol) <span class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://anno.onb.ac.at/cgi-content/anno-buch?apm=0&amp;aid=1000001&amp;bd=0001878&amp;teil=0203&amp;seite=00000228&amp;zoom=1">Hofmeister's <i>Monatsbericht</i> (1878), p. 228</a></span>. 
</li><li>The last volume of this series was found missing in the uploader's library, but is available in another edition <a href="/wiki/3_Duos_for_2_Violins,_Op.39_(Spohr,_Louis)" title="3 Duos for 2 Violins, Op.39 (Spohr, Louis)">here</a>.
</li><li>The books are progressive order of difficulty.
</li></ul>
